Frequently Asked Questions

How does automatic trip detection work?

Milo Go uses GPS to detect when you start driving. Enable "Auto-Detect Trips" on the main screen and make sure location access is set to "Always" in your iPhone Settings. Trips are recorded in the background automatically.

What IRS mileage rates does Milo Go use?

Milo Go automatically applies the correct IRS standard mileage rate for each category and tax year: Business (72.5¢/mi for 2026), Medical and Military Moving (21¢/mi), and Charity (14¢/mi, set by statute). Rates are applied based on the year each trip was recorded, so historical trips always use the correct rate.

Can I import trips from another mileage app?

Yes. Milo Go imports CSV exports from Everlance, MileIQ, Stride, TripLog, QuickBooks Self-Employed, Driversnote, Gridwise, Hurdlr, MileageWise, Falcon Expenses, Magica, and most other mileage tracking apps. Tap the menu button and select "Import Trips."

Does Milo Go sync across devices?

Yes. Your trip data syncs securely across all your Apple devices through iCloud. No account or sign-up required — it works automatically if iCloud is enabled on your device.

How do I export my trips for tax filing?

Tap the menu button on the main screen and choose "Share CSV" or "Share PDF Report." You can filter by year, month, or category before exporting. Reports can be shared directly with your accountant via email, AirDrop, or any sharing method.

Is my data private?

Yes. Milo Go does not collect any personal data, has no third-party analytics, and no ads. Your trip data is stored on your device and synced only through your personal iCloud account. No one else has access to it.

How do I classify trips?

Swipe right on any trip to choose Personal, Business, or tap "More" for Medical, Charity, and Military Moving categories. You can also use Quick Classify mode from the menu to swipe through unclassified trips one by one, or tap "Select" to batch classify multiple trips at once.
